Points Rummy

Points rummy is one of the fastest ways to play the game. As the name suggests, you play for points and the goal of the game is to arrange your cards into sequences and sets so you ultimately end up with zero points. The first player to make a valid declaration ultimately wins.

Every card in this variant comes with its own pre-assigned value (or points). Ace – 10 points. King, Queen, and Jack (also known as face cards)– 10 points each. Printed joker/wild card joker – 0 points. Numerical cards – The same points as the value printed on the cards.

Here’s how scoring works:

  • If you drop from the game in your first turn, then you will be penalised with 20 points.
  • The penalty for dropping from a game after that is 40 points.
  • The penalty for an invalid declaration is 80 points.

Because you have chosen to play a practice game, you won’t lose any money on these games. However, when you win, you will also not win exciting cash prizes.

Pool Rummy

You will be able to choose a Pool table on RummyTime when you log in. There are three types of sub-variants available on RummyTime. They are:

  • 101 Pool
  • 61 Pool
  • 201 Pool

Pool Rummy is generally played over multiple rounds. The goal is to accumulate the least number of points. If you choose to play 61 Pool, then the first player to reach 61 points will be eliminated. The same logic applies to 101 Pool and 201 Pool. The last player who hasn’t been eliminated is declared the winner.

Every round comes to an end when a player makes a valid declaration. That player will have zero points. The other players are assigned points based on the cards they possess. Much like Points Rummy, this is how the scoring works:

  • Ace – 10 points.
  • King, Queen, and Jack (also known as face cards)– 10 points each.
  • Printed joker/wild card joker – 0 points.
  • Numerical cards – The same points as the value printed on the cards.

Here’s how players will be penalised if they drop during the game:

Pool variant First drop Middle drop
61 Pool 15 points 30 points
101 Pool 20 points 40 points
201 Pool 25 points 50 points

And, this is how the penalty is assigned for invalid declarations:

Pool variant Invalid declaration penalty
61 Pool 60
101 Pool 80
201 Pool 80

It should be noted that Pool rummy is a variant where players can rejoin a game. This can happen If no player on the table has points over 44 in 61 Pool rummy, 79 in 101 Pool rummy and 174 in 201 Pool rummy.

Deals Rummy

In Deals Rummy, players agree to a pre-decided number of deals.

Chips are distributed before every game and it should be noted that chips are distributed even for free and practice games.

  • In 2 player games, 160 chips are distributed to each player.
  • In 6 player games 480 chips are distributed to each player.

Please note that the number of chips you receive depends on the number of pre-decided deals. So, if you are joining a 2-deal game, then you will receive 160 chips. At the end of every deal, the player who makes the first valid declaration will receive the chips from all the other players. The player with the most number of chips at the end of all the deals will be declared the winner.

You can play as many free Deals games as you want on RummyTime. Do keep practicing until you feel ready to play and win cash games.
