Rummy Game Rules and How to Play

The objective of rummy is to win by making a valid declaration. All players have to arrange their cards into sequences and sets. To make a valid declaration, they will need at least two sequences of which one of them has to be a pure sequence. The first player to make a valid declaration which satisfies all rummy rules is the winner.

How Scoring Works

  • Printed jokers and wild card jokers - 0 points
  • Face cards such as jack, queen, and king - 10 points
  • Idle ace cards that are not part of a set or a sequence - 10 points

How to Play Rummy

There are typically either 2 player games or 6 player games. Generally, it is played with 1 to 2 standard decks of 52 cards in 13 card rummy format where all players are dealt with 13 cards. One card is randomly selected and declared as the wild card Joker.

There is an open pile and a closed pile. When it is their turn, players have to draw one card and discard one card. On the RummyTime platform, you can arrange all your cards using the “Sort” option. You can draw a card from the closed or the open pile by tapping on them and then discard one card by using the “Discard” option.

Rummy Tips and Tricks for Smart Gameplay

Listed below are the rummy tips and tricks which are often practiced by pro and experienced players.

Form Pure Sequences First

Your first goal when you start the game is to make a pure sequence. A pure sequence is made without the help of a joker (although if a wild card joker fits in the sequential order then it can be used to form a pure sequence). No valid declaration can happen without a pure sequence, so that’s the top priority.

Discard High-Value Cards

Discard all idly high-value cards that are not part of a sequence or a set. This is to avoid losing by a large margin on the off chance someone else ends up making a valid declaration before you. Avoid picking up from the discard pile to avoid fishing

Use Your Joker Wisely

Instead of slotting the joker anywhere, make sure you slot them between high-value cards to create sequences and sets. This way you will avoid losing by a large margin in case someone else makes a valid declaration before you get the chance to do so.
